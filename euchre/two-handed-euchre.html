Two-Handed Euchre Cheat Sheet

Euchre for 2 players. Same 24-card deck and card rankings as classic euchre, but with a different deal, trick-based bidding (similar to Spades), and 12 tricks per round. Each player has a hand plus face-up and face-down cards in front of them; you may play from your hand or from your face-up cards.

Source: Euchre.com – Two-Handed Euchre

Setup

  • Deck: 9–A only (24 cards)
  • Players: 2
  • Card rankings and trump order same as standard euchre (right bower, left bower)

Game Flow

1. Deal

All 24 cards are used. Each player ends up with:

  • 4 cards in hand (private)
  • 4 cards face-down in a row in front of them
  • 4 cards face-up on top of the face-down row

Dealing method: dealer gives one card to opponent’s layout, one to their own, and so on—building the face-down piles first, then placing face-up cards on top. Finally each player receives 4 cards for their hand. Both players can see all 8 exposed cards (their own 4 face-up and the opponent’s 4 face-up).

2. Bidding

  • Bidding is like Spades: each player bids how many tricks they will take. Minimum bid is 7 tricks.
  • Non-dealer bids first.
  • Consider your hand and all 8 visible cards before bidding.
  • The player who bids higher names the trump suit. Only that player (the “maker”) can score points for the round.

3. Playing

  • Non-dealer leads to the first trick.
  • 12 tricks are played (all 24 cards).
  • On your turn you play one card either from your hand or from one of your face-up cards.
  • When you play a face-up card, turn over the face-down card underneath it; that card is now visible and can be played on a later trick.
  • Follow suit if you can; otherwise you may play any card. Trump beats non-trump; otherwise highest card of the lead suit wins.
  • Winner of each trick leads to the next.

4. Scoring

Only the maker scores for the round:

  • Maker wins 7 tricks → 1 point
  • 8 tricks → 2 points
  • 9 tricks → 3 points
  • … up to 12 tricks → 6 points

If the maker takes fewer than 7 tricks, they score 0 for the round.

Game end: First player to reach 10 points wins.

Trump Order (Low → High)

Same as standard euchre: jack of trump is highest (“right bower”), other jack of same color is second (“left bower”) and counts as trump.

♦ Diamonds: 9♦ 10♦ Q♦ K♦ A♦ J♥ J♦ (J♥ is second highest diamonds) ♥ Hearts: 9♥ 10♥ Q♥ K♥ A♥ J♦ J♥ (J♦ is second highest hearts) ♠ Spades: 9♠ 10♠ Q♠ K♠ A♠ J♣ J♠ (J♣ is second highest spades) ♣ Clubs: 9♣ 10♣ Q♣ K♣ A♣ J♠ J♣ (J♠ is second highest clubs)

Strategy Tips

  • Keep some cards out of sight. If everything you have left is visible, your opponent can plan against it.
  • Sometimes it’s better to play a high face-up card and expose the one under it—that card might be trump or otherwise useful.
  • Use the visible layout (yours and opponent’s) when bidding and when deciding which card to play.
  • Leading a non-trump ace is often strong; pull trump when you have control.

Other Variation

Another 2-handed style: deal 5 cards to each player plus 3 extra cards that stay hidden until after trump is chosen. Bidding works like classic euchre (order up / call trump). After trump is set, both players look at their 3 extra cards and choose the best 5-card hand from their 8 cards. If the maker goes alone, they may not use the 3 extra cards and can score more points for a march.
