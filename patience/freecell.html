Freecell Cheat Sheet

One deck (52 cards), all face-up. Build four foundations up by suit (A→K). Use four free cells to hold single cards and eight tableau columns built down in alternating colors. Unlike Klondike there is no stock—every card is visible from the start. Almost every deal is winnable.

Setup

  • Deck: one standard 52-card deck
  • Players: 1
  • Tableau: 8 columns, all cards face-up. Columns 1–4 have 7 cards each; columns 5–8 have 6 cards each.
  • Foundations: 4 empty piles (build up by suit, A→K).
  • Free cells: 4 empty slots; each may hold one card at a time.

Game Flow

Foundations

  • Move Aces to the foundations as they become available.
  • Build up by suit in order: A, 2, 3, … K. Only the next higher card of the same suit may be added.
  • Goal: Get all 52 cards onto the four foundations.

Tableau

  • Build down in alternating colors (e.g. black 5 on red 6, red Q on black K).
  • Only the top card of a column (or the top card of a valid sequence) may be moved. You may move a single card or a sequence of cards that form a valid descending alternating-color run.
  • Empty column: Any single card or valid sequence may be placed in an empty tableau column.

Free cells

  • Each free cell holds one card. Use them to temporarily park a card so you can reach the card below it or build a different sequence.
  • Any card may be placed in an empty free cell. Only the top card of a column (or the leading card of a sequence you’re moving) may be moved into a free cell.

Moving sequences

  • You can move a sequence of cards as a block only if you have enough empty free cells and empty columns to unpack them one card at a time (e.g. move one card to a free cell, then the next onto the destination, etc.). With 4 empty free cells, you can move a sequence of up to 5 cards. Empty tableau columns also increase how long a sequence you can move.

Winning

  • You win when all four foundations are complete (Ace through King in each suit).

Strategy tips

  • Move Aces and low cards to the foundations early to free space.
  • Use free cells to unblock columns; avoid filling all four if you don’t need to.
  • Empty columns are very powerful—they let you move longer sequences; try to clear one when you can.
  • Plan a few moves ahead; since everything is visible, you can look for sequences that will build toward the foundations.
