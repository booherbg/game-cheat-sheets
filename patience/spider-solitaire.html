Spider Solitaire Cheat Sheet

Spider uses two decks (104 cards). Build descending sequences (K down to A) on the tableau; only same-suit complete sequences (K→A) may be removed. When you're stuck, deal a new row of 10 cards from the stock—but you cannot deal while any column is empty. Win by clearing all eight sequences.

Standard rules; Bicycle Cards uses an alternate final deal (see Dealing from the stock).

Setup

  • Deck: two standard 52-card decks (104 cards); jokers removed.
  • Players: 1
  • Tableau: 10 columns. 54 cards are dealt: the first 4 columns get 6 cards each, the next 6 columns get 5 cards each. Only the top card of each column is face-up; the rest are face-down.
  • Stock: Remaining 50 cards, face-down.
  • Foundation: Completed same-suit sequences (K down to A) are removed from the tableau and set aside; no separate foundation piles—you "discard" full sequences.

Game Flow

Building on the tableau

  • Build down in descending order (e.g. 9 on 10, Q on K). Suits may be mixed when building—you can put any 8 on any 9.
  • You may move a single card or a complete sequence of cards (in descending order, any suits) as a unit, as long as the move is valid (the bottom card of the moved group goes on a card one rank higher).
  • Only a King (or a sequence headed by a King) may be placed in an empty column.
  • When you move all face-up cards off a column, turn the next card face-up.

Removing sequences

  • When a column has a complete descending sequence in one suit from King down to Ace, that sequence is removed from the tableau (set aside). That frees the column (or the cards under it). You do not build "foundations" on the side—you simply remove the 13 cards from play.
  • Goal: Remove all eight sequences (2 per suit in the standard game).

Dealing from the stock

  • You start with 50 cards in the stock. When you have no (or no more) moves, you may deal one new row from the stock: one card face-up onto the top of each of the 10 columns. Each deal uses 10 cards (one per column).
  • Rule: You cannot deal if any column is empty. You must fill empty columns with cards or sequences before you are allowed to deal from the stock.
  • Standard rule: There are 5 deals of 10 cards each. All 50 cards enter play, so the game is winnable. After 5 deals the stock is empty.
  • Alternate rule (e.g. Bicycle Cards): Some rule sets say the final deal is only 4 cards—placed on the first four columns—leaving 6 cards in the stock that are never dealt. That variant can make the game unwinnable if key cards are among those 6; use the standard rule above if you want a fair, winnable game.

Winning

  • You win when all eight same-suit sequences (K→A) have been removed from the tableau.

Difficulty variants

Rules above are for 4 suits (hearts, diamonds, clubs, spades). Same two decks for all; only what "suit" means when removing a sequence changes:

  • 4 suits: Suit = actual suit. (Standard—rules above.) Hardest.
  • 2 suits: Suit = red (hearts + diamonds) or black (spades + clubs). A removable sequence must be all red or all black. Intermediate.
  • 1 suit: Suit doesn't matter—any K→A run may be removed. Easiest.

Strategy tips

  • Empty columns are powerful—but you must fill them before dealing. Prefer moving sequences that free face-down cards.
  • Try to expose and turn face-down cards early.
  • Building long mixed sequences can block cards; when possible, keep same-suit runs together so they can be removed as a unit.
  • Avoid dealing until you've made every useful move; the deal adds cards and can make the position harder.
